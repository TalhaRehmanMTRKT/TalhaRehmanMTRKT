Agent-based Grid Operation

Developing an agent-based framework for smart grid operation, leveraging multi-agent systems for distributed energy resource management and grid optimization. The work explores how cooperative agents negotiate dispatch, balance reserves, and coordinate flexible loads in real time, with the longer-term goal of integrating learning-based controllers into hierarchical grid architectures.

LSTM for Flood & Drought Forecasting

LSTM-based deep learning models for time series forecasting of floods and droughts. Completed as my Final Year Project at Namal University (Undergraduate), under the joint supervision of the Center of Artificial Intelligence and Big Data (Namal) and Water Informatics Technology (WIT, LUMS). The project explored data-driven hydrological forecasting from historical river-flow and climate signals.
